Occidental Mindoro

Occidental Mindoro is a province in the Philippines located on the western part of Mindoro Island. Known as the "Home of the Tamaraw," it is famous for its rich biodiversity and the stunning Apo Reef Natural Park, one of the world's most significant coral reef systems. The province features a diverse landscape of rugged mountain ranges, fertile plains, and pristine beaches, making it an ideal destination for eco-tourism and outdoor adventure. Its capital, Mamburao, and other coastal towns serve as gateways to vibrant marine life and the unique cultural heritage of the indigenous Mangyan people.
